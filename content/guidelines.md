Purpose

This document defines the principles and structure for generating questions for Know-Thyself, a private daily writing game.

The goal of every question:

> Increase self-clarity over time through short, honest writing.



This is not coaching.
Insight emerges from repetition, pattern recognition, and presence.


---

Core Tone

Primary tone: introspective and educational

Secondary tones, used lightly:

Philosophical

Gently playful


Every question must:

Reveal something about the self

Avoid moralizing

Avoid advice disguised as inquiry

Avoid “should” energy


Questions should feel:

Direct

Human

Clear

Non-performative

Not clever for the sake of cleverness



---

Foundational Rule

If structure conflicts with flow, flow wins.

The practice is primary.
The system is scaffolding.


---

Structural Model

Standalone Design

Each question must stand on its own.

Users join at different times. There is no linear onboarding progression.

Continuity is created through:

Flexible batches (1–5 questions)

Light thematic coherence within batches

Optionally surfacing previous answers from the same batch



---

Tags (Themes Only)

Tags represent themes only.
They are not used for intensity, format, or type.

Keep the system minimal and stable.

Initial Tag Set (8 total)

1. Identity


2. Values


3. Emotions


4. Fears


5. Relationships


6. Habits


7. Meaning


8. Patterns



Rules:

Each question must have 1–2 tags maximum

Do not exceed 10 total tags without quarterly review

Tags may be refined, merged, or removed during review

No micro-tags

No hierarchy for now


Tags are invisible scaffolding, not a user-facing taxonomy.


---

Prompt Length Guidelines

90% of Prompts

Short. Direct. Clean.

8–16 words

One idea

Clear entry point

Expected answer: 2–6 sentences


Prompts must feel answerable even on a busy day.


---

10% of Prompts — Optional Deep Dive

Rules:

Every day starts with a short core prompt

On ~10% of days, include an optional deeper extension

Deep dive is clearly marked (icon TBD)

Deep dive is optional, never required


Structure:

Short Prompt
Optional Deep Dive

The deep dive expands depth, not pressure.


---

When Nothing Comes to Mind

Some days, the prompt won’t resonate.

Instead of skipping, we offer a gentle fallback.

Button label:

> “Nothing comes to mind? Do this instead.”



Fallback mode:

Free reflection.

Instruction:

> If today’s question doesn’t spark anything, pause and write whatever is present right now. No structure. No performance. Just the moment.



Free reflection counts fully as presence.

Prompt-writing and free-writing are philosophically equal.
Showing up is the metric.


---

Unique vs Evergreen Questions

Baseline split: 70 / 30 (Provisional)

70% Unique (shown once globally)

30% Evergreen (may reappear occasionally)


This ratio is a starting hypothesis, not doctrine.
Quarterly review may adjust it.

Unique Questions

Time-sensitive

Contextual

Specific framing that loses power if repeated


Evergreen Questions

Identity-level

Cyclical themes

Pattern-based

May reveal different answers over time



---

Interweaving Rhythm

Light structure, not rigid scheduling.

Recommended pattern:

One batch (1–5 themed unique questions)

Then 1–2 evergreen questions as integration

Then next batch


Psychological rhythm:

Depth → Integration → New Direction

No calendar rigidity required.

Hard-Coded Example Questions (First 4)

Unique #1

What are you pretending not to know right now?

Unique #2

Evergreen #1

Evergreen #2

These define tone and depth baseline.


---

Emotional Guardrails

This is not therapy.

Questions must avoid:

Forced trauma excavation

Direct probing of unresolved childhood wounds

Emotional destabilization without consent


Depth is welcome.
Re-traumatization is not.

Prompts should invite reflection, not exposure. Default bias toward observation, values, and gratitude.

Research backs this up: forced stressful disclosure can backfire (see `research/gpt-research-4-2-26`). Favor observation, values, and gratitude early on.


---

Public-Share Filter

Because the question itself may be shared publicly, every prompt must also pass this filter:

- Safe to publish as a standalone question card
- Invites curiosity or recognition, not confession pressure
- Never implies the answer itself should be shared

Shareability is a bonus, not the primary goal. Private usefulness comes first.


---

Presence Philosophy

A day counts only when the user clicks “Finalize.”

Drafting does not count.
Reading does not count.

Presence = deliberate showing up.

The metric is participation, not performance.


---

Content Generation Strategy

We do not pre-generate a year of content.

We generate incrementally.

Approach:

Start with a 30-question seed bank

Expand gradually

Let lived experience shape future prompts

Revise guidelines as patterns emerge


The guideline evolves with the practice.
